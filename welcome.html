Authors

Most prominent authors

Ahmed Khaled Tawfik (June 10, 1962 – April 2, 2018) was a university professor, physician, writer, author, and translator from Egypt. He is considered the first Arab author in the field of horror literature and the most renowned in the fields of youth literature, fantasy, and science fiction. He was nicknamed 'The Godfather'.

Ahmed Khaled Tawfik

Egyptian writer

Naguib Mahfouz Abdel Aziz Ibrahim Ahmed Al-Basha (December 11, 1911 – August 30, 2006) was an Egyptian writer. He is considered the first Egyptian and Arab to win the Nobel Prize in Literature. Naguib Mahfouz wrote from the 1930s until 2004. All of his novels take place in Egypt and feature a recurring theme, which is the neighborhood that represents the world.

Naguib Mahfouz

Egyptian writer

Ahmed Mourad (born February 14, 1978) is an Egyptian writer, novelist, screenwriter, photographer, and graphic designer. He graduated from Lycée Liberté in Bab El Louq in 1996 before joining the Higher Institute of Cinema to study cinematography, graduating in 2001 at the top of his class. His graduation films "The Lost Ones - The Three Papers - And On the Seventh Day" won awards for short films at festivals in England, France, and Ukraine.

Ahmed Mourad
